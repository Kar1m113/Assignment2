Where do refugees come from?

Figure 1 - Choropleth map

The Choropleth map above is showing the distribution of

refugees amongst countries that they come from with darkest being

the highest number of Asylum Seekers that left that country.

Looking at the map, it can be seen that the

countries with the most refugees leaving is

Cuba, Guatemala and Honduras, with the total number of refugees

leaving are 1687480, 1571130 and 1287800 respectively.

This data illustrates the ethnicities of most refugees are

from countries in Central America.

Note. These values have been normalised with a log scale.

Figure 2 - Choropleth map

The Choropleth map above is showing the distribution of

refugees amongst the countries that they flee to.

The map shows that the highest number of Asylum Seekers

go to countries like Colombia, Trindad and Tobago and Jordan.

The number of Asylum Seekers leaving these countries are

223450, 180380 and 109690 respectively.

This data presents that refugees are seeking refuge in countries

that are near by their home due to the

lack of quality of life, caused by wars or poverty.

Note. These values have been normalised with a log scale.

The line chart to the left is a timeline from

2012 to 2022, showing the number of refugees that

entered a country each year.

The 5 countries that were used for the line chart are

Australia, Malaysia, United States of America, Great Britain and Netherlands.

The visualisation shows that all countries are having increasing number

of refugees entering their countries. Especially for european countries,

Great Britain has had a 140% in total number of

refugees entering from 2021 to 2022. Also, the Netherlands has

increased by 119% of its total number of refugees in 2021.

This behaviour can also be seen in the USA,

where its total increased by 7%.

However, this drastic increase does not apply to Australia and

Malaysia. The total number of refugees in these countries

has plateaued in recent years.

Figure 5 - Stacked Bar Chart

The Stacked Bar Chart illustrates the number of refugees in

certain occupations, for both male and female refugees

From the visualisation, there are certain occupations where

it is majority of males with that occupation or females.

This can be seen for Automotive Trade Workers,

where only 363 females have that occupation compared to

27,890 males. This gives a ratio of 1 female: 77 males

This distribution can be seen in other occupations such as

Construction workers and Rail/Road Drivers.

However, females are dominating other occupations such as

Carers, Professional Educators and Health Professionals
